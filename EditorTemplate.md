# Cross-AI Research Workflow (Harness Claude / Gemini / ChatGPT)
This workflow applies a multi-AI "harness" technique to prior art (novelty/FTO) research, using Claude, Gemini, and ChatGPT together to reduce the risk of missed or biased findings.

- **Phase 1 — Parallel Search:** All three AIs receive the identical base prompt, run independently (no cross-referencing), and are each assigned a differentiated emphasis — Claude for deep claim-element mapping, Gemini for broad and non-English patent coverage, ChatGPT for analogous-field searches — to maximize combined coverage rather than redundancy.
- **Phase 2 — Rotated Verification:** Roles rotate so each AI checks another AI's findings (never its own), confirming citation accuracy, publication details, and relevance scoring.
- **Phase 3 — Consolidation:** Verified results merge into one master table; conflicts are resolved by rules (consensus finds are high-confidence, single-AI finds are flagged as unique, disputed scores go to human review, unverifiable citations are discarded). A final gap-analysis pass catches claim elements no AI addressed.
- **Phase 4 — Report Writing:** Findings are compiled into a structured report — methodology, consolidated table, per-reference analysis, cross-validation notes, gaps, and conclusion — with human fact-checking of all citations mandatory before use.

**Key principles:** preserve independence during initial search, rotate (never self-verify) during validation, always confirm AI-cited references against real databases, and leave final novelty/FTO judgment to a qualified patent professional.
